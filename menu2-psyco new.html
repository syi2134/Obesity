PHYSICAL CAUSES TO OBESITY

STRESS/PRESSURE
Psychological causes such as stress and pressure due to lack of sleep or work have shown to increase the chances of obesity. In one study, researchers followed one thousand children from birth to age 32 and found that each hour reduction in sleep during childhood resulted in a 50% higher risk of obesity at age 32. Due to being tired and stressed from lack of sleep, people tend to do less exercise and eat more.
CHOICE OF FOODS
Television is a very strong obesity factor due to the constant exposure to unhealthy food and beverages. It may influence what people eat. Other influences include peer pressure or bullying.
